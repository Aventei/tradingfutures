Beyond Guesswork

The Science of Market Analysis

"Traders need to use in-depth technical analysis. This includes studying past market data and backtesting strategies, and understanding market psychology to anticipate future price movements." - Funded Futures Network

Joshua tracks "volume, order flow, and liquidity to keep his edge on the market."

Key Technical Analysis Components

  • Price action: The movement of price over time, forming patterns and structures
  • Technical indicators: Mathematical calculations based on price, volume, or open interest
  • Chart patterns: Recurring formations that suggest potential future price movements
  • Support and resistance levels: Price areas where buying or selling pressure has historically emerged
  • Volume analysis: Examining trading volume to confirm price movements

Combining Technical and Fundamental Analysis

According to the article "How to Combine Fundamental and Technical Analysis in Forex," the most effective approach integrates both technical and fundamental perspectives:

  • Fundamental analysis determines the overall direction and potential
  • Technical analysis identifies optimal entry and exit points
  • Together, they provide a more complete picture than either approach alone

Example: Multi-Timeframe Analysis

Successful traders often analyze markets across multiple timeframes to gain perspective:

  1. Higher timeframe: Identify the overall trend direction (daily/weekly charts)
  2. Intermediate timeframe: Spot potential trading opportunities (4-hour charts)
  3. Lower timeframe: Fine-tune entry and exit points (15-minute/1-hour charts)

This approach helps traders align with the larger trend while finding optimal entry points, rather than simply trying to "buy low and sell high" without context.

  1. Hypothesis formation: Developing a potential edge based on market observations
  2. Strategy definition: Creating specific rules for entries, exits, and position sizing

Technical Analysis in Action

Traders use multiple indicators and patterns to identify potential entry and exit points, not just "buy low, sell high" guesswork.

Common Technical Indicators

Indicator Purpose Best Used For
Moving Averages Identify trend direction and potential support/resistance Trending markets
RSI (Relative Strength Index) Measure momentum and identify overbought/oversold conditions Range-bound markets
MACD (Moving Average Convergence Divergence) Identify momentum shifts and potential trend changes All market conditions
Bollinger Bands Measure volatility and potential price extremes Volatile markets
Volume Profile Identify price levels with significant trading activity All market conditions
